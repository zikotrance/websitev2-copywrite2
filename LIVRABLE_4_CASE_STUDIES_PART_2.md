### Project Overview

**Titre:**
> About the Project

**Client Background:**

The client is a regional real estate development company known for thoughtfully designed urban infill projects that combine residential, commercial, and retail spaces in walkable, community-oriented developments. Their portfolio includes seven completed mixed-use projects across major metropolitan areas, each characterized by high-quality materials, contemporary design, and sustainable construction practices that have earned recognition from industry organizations and community groups alike.

The Urban Quarter development represents their most ambitious project to date: a 450,000 square foot mixed-use complex comprising 280 residential units, 85,000 square feet of Class A office space, 25,000 square feet of street-level retail, and extensive amenity areas including a rooftop terrace, fitness center, coworking lounge, and event spaces. The development occupies a full city block in a rapidly evolving urban neighborhood, serving as an anchor for ongoing neighborhood revitalization.

The design team, led by a nationally recognized architecture firm, specified extensive use of architectural glass throughout the project to create visual transparency, maximize natural light, and establish a contemporary aesthetic that would distinguish the development from conventional mixed-use properties in the market.

**Project Scope:**

The architectural glass scope encompassed multiple applications across the development, each with specific design requirements and performance criteria. Glass partitions were required for office floor demising walls and conference rooms, creating acoustically separated spaces while maintaining visual openness and natural light penetration. Glass railings were needed for residential corridors, amenity terraces, and the rooftop lounge, providing safety without obstructing views. Glass canopies protected building entrances and walkway connections between structures. Interior glass features included a dramatic three-story feature wall in the residential lobby and glass-enclosed elevator lobbies on each floor.

The total scope exceeded 15,000 square feet of architectural glass across various configurations, requiring coordination between multiple installation teams and careful scheduling to align with the overall construction timeline.

**Key Requirements:**

The design and construction team established rigorous requirements for the architectural glass systems. Structural performance was essential—all glass systems needed to meet or exceed building code requirements for wind loads, impact resistance, and safety, with engineering documentation suitable for building department approval. Aesthetic consistency was critical—the glass systems needed to maintain a cohesive visual language across different applications, from lobby partitions to rooftop railings. Durability was paramount—the developer wanted products that would maintain their appearance and performance for the long term, avoiding the degradation and replacement issues they had experienced with previous projects. Coordination capability was necessary—given the complexity of the overall project, the glass supplier needed to work effectively with the general contractor, structural engineer, and various subcontractors.

Budget considerations were important but secondary to quality—the developer recognized that architectural glass was a signature element of the design and was willing to invest appropriately to achieve the desired result.

---

### The Challenge

**Titre:**
> The Challenge: Coordinating Complex Glass Systems at Scale

**Problem Statement:**

Mixed-use developments present unique challenges for architectural glass procurement and installation. Unlike single-use projects where glass requirements are relatively uniform, mixed-use developments demand multiple glass systems with different specifications, performance requirements, and installation sequences—all while maintaining design cohesion and meeting aggressive construction schedules.

The Urban Quarter development amplified these challenges through its scale, design complexity, and the involvement of multiple stakeholders with different priorities. The residential component required glass systems that met residential building codes and could withstand the wear patterns of daily residential use. The office component required glass systems that met commercial building standards and supported tenant improvement flexibility. The amenity spaces required glass systems that made design statements while meeting safety requirements for public assembly spaces. The exterior applications required glass systems that could withstand weather exposure while meeting energy code requirements.

**Previous Project Issues:**

The development company had experienced significant coordination challenges with architectural glass on previous projects, despite working with reputable suppliers. On a 200,000 square foot office building completed three years earlier, the glass partition systems had required extensive field modifications to fit actual conditions, creating delays and cost overruns. The glass railing systems on that project had developed corrosion at attachment points within two years, requiring remediation that disrupted tenant operations.

On a residential project completed five years earlier, the glass supplier had failed to coordinate effectively with the general contractor, resulting in delivery timing that didn't match installation windows and created costly on-site storage requirements. The glass canopies on that project had experienced seal failures that allowed water infiltration, damaging adjacent building components and creating liability exposure.

These experiences had taught the development company that architectural glass procurement required more than competitive pricing and product specifications—it required a partner who understood construction coordination, could manage complex logistics, and would stand behind their products over the long term.

**Business Impact:**

The stakes for the Urban Quarter project were particularly high. The development represented a significant capital investment and was positioned as a flagship property that would influence the company's reputation and future development opportunities. The architectural glass systems were highly visible design elements that would shape first impressions for prospective tenants, residents, and retail customers.

Failures or issues with the glass systems would be immediately apparent and could undermine the premium positioning that justified the project's economics. The development team was determined to avoid the problems they had experienced on previous projects and establish a new standard for quality and coordination.

### The HILO Solution

**Titre:**
> Why HILO Was Selected

The development company's construction team conducted a comprehensive evaluation of architectural glass suppliers, with particular emphasis on coordination capabilities, engineering support, and long-term reliability. The evaluation process included review of technical capabilities and product specifications, analysis of project management and logistics processes, verification of engineering documentation and code compliance expertise, assessment of warranty coverage and claims history, and reference checks with developers of comparable projects.

HILO was one of four suppliers invited to participate in detailed discussions with the project team, including the developer, architect, structural engineer, and general contractor. Each supplier was asked to present their approach to the project's specific challenges and demonstrate their coordination capabilities.

HILO emerged as the clear choice based on several decisive factors. The integrated engineering support provided in-house structural calculations and PE-stamped documentation, reducing coordination burden on the project team and accelerating approval processes. The comprehensive project management approach included dedicated coordination with the general contractor and proactive communication about scheduling, deliveries, and installation sequences. The 5-year comprehensive warranty covering glass, hardware, seals, and installation workmanship provided protection that competing suppliers couldn't match.

HILO's experience with mixed-use developments was demonstrated through references from three comparable projects, including site visits that allowed the development team to see installed products and speak with property managers about long-term performance. The competitive pricing was within 5% of the lowest-cost alternative while providing significantly superior warranty coverage and support capabilities.

**Products Selected:**

HILO worked closely with the design team to develop a product mix that met each application's requirements while maintaining aesthetic consistency. ### Implementation

**Titre:**
> Complex Coordination Across Multiple Phases

**Process Overview:**

The 14-month glass installation timeline was structured around the overall construction schedule, which included phased occupancy across residential, office, and retail components. HILO's project management approach evolved through five distinct phases to maintain alignment with construction progress.

Phase 1 focused on pre-construction coordination, including detailed measurement verification, engineering calculations, and shop drawing development. This phase also established communication protocols and scheduling frameworks that would guide the entire project. Phase 2 addressed early installations including entrance canopies and exterior railings, which needed to be complete before facade work could proceed. Phase 3 covered residential corridor railings and lobby feature elements, coordinated with interior fit-out timing. Phase 4 installed office partition systems, timed to align with the office building's core-and-shell completion and prospective tenant tours. Phase 5 completed final elements including rooftop terrace railings and amenity space partitions, coordinated with overall project completion and certificate of occupancy timing.

**Coordination Excellence:**

The coordination complexity of this project required HILO to establish dedicated project management resources that went beyond typical supplier involvement. A senior project manager was assigned as the primary point of contact for all glass-related coordination, attending weekly construction meetings and maintaining continuous communication with the general contractor's scheduling team. Engineering documentation was prepared and submitted ahead of schedule, allowing building department review without creating delays. Delivery scheduling was integrated with the overall logistics plan, ensuring that glass materials arrived when needed without creating storage congestion on the active construction site.

The coordination investment paid dividends throughout the project. When the construction schedule shifted due to factors unrelated to glass, HILO adjusted production and delivery timing accordingly, preventing the mismatch between material availability and installation windows that had plagued previous projects. When field conditions required minor modifications to original designs, HILO's engineering team responded within 24-48 hours with revised documentation, maintaining project momentum.

**Quality Control:**

Quality control was maintained through multiple mechanisms. Factory inspections by the development team's quality assurance consultants verified that products met specifications before shipping. On-site inspections verified that installations met HILO's standards and the project's requirements. Documentation was maintained for each installation, creating a complete record that would support future warranty claims or modifications.

The final punch list process identified fewer than 30 items across all glass installations—significantly fewer than the development company had experienced on previous projects of comparable scale. All items were resolved within two weeks of project completion, with no disputes about responsibility or warranty coverage.

### Project Overview

**Titre:**
> About the Project

**Client Background:**

The Langford Hotel is a 78-room boutique property located in a historic building in the heart of a vibrant Canadian city. Originally constructed in 1928 as an office building, the property was converted to a boutique hotel in 2004, capitalizing on the building's historic character, prime location, and architectural details to create a distinctive hospitality experience. The hotel has built a loyal following among travelers who appreciate its blend of historic charm, contemporary amenities, and personalized service.

The hotel's ownership group acquired the property in 2018 with a commitment to ongoing investment in guest experience and building systems. Their renovation approach has emphasized quality improvements that honor the building's historic character while meeting the expectations of today's travelers. Previous projects included mechanical system upgrades, window replacements that matched historic profiles, and lobby restoration that returned original architectural elements to prominence.

The 2024 guest room renovation represented the most comprehensive update since the original conversion, encompassing new case goods, refreshed soft goods, updated bathrooms, and replacement of the medicine cabinets that had been installed during the 2004 conversion.

**Project Scope:**

The renovation scope included all 78 guest rooms across the hotel's five floors, with each room receiving a new medicine cabinet as part of the bathroom refresh. The total requirement was 156 medicine cabinets, accounting for rooms with single vanity configurations as well as several suites with double vanity bathrooms.

The project timeline was constrained by the hotel's decision to remain open during renovation, requiring phased floor-by-floor work that limited each phase to approximately three weeks. This approach maintained revenue during the renovation but created scheduling complexity and required precise coordination between the renovation team and hotel operations.

**Key Requirements:**

The ownership group established specific requirements for the medicine cabinet selection that reflected their quality-first approach and the property's unique characteristics. Historical appropriateness was essential—the cabinets needed to complement the building's 1920s character while providing contemporary functionality, avoiding the jarring contrast between period-appropriate design and obviously modern fixtures. Durability was paramount—the owners were determined to break the replacement cycle they had experienced with the original cabinets, which had required multiple repairs and replacements over the property's 20-year hotel history.

Integrated lighting was required—the existing cabinets had lighting that was considered adequate when installed but now fell short of guest expectations and energy efficiency requirements. Recessed installation was necessary given the bathroom layouts and the desire to maximize usable counter space. Finally, the supplier needed to coordinate with the renovation schedule, delivering products in phases that aligned with floor-by-floor renovation timing.

---

### The Challenge

**Titre:**
> The Challenge: Ending a 20-Year Replacement Cycle

**Problem Statement:**

The medicine cabinets installed during the hotel's 2004 conversion had become a persistent source of maintenance issues and guest complaints. While the cabinets had initially appeared adequate, problems began emerging within the first few years and accelerated as the cabinets aged.

The failure pattern was predictable and frustrating. Mirror delamination created black edges that expanded over time, creating unsightly spots that guests noticed immediately. Shelf sagging made the cabinets functionally unusable for storing anything beyond lightweight toiletries. Door alignment problems developed as hinges loosened and the cabinet structure shifted, creating gaps and preventing smooth closure. Lighting failures became increasingly common as the original LED components—cutting-edge when installed but now obsolete—failed without available replacement parts. Moisture infiltration around the cabinet perimeter caused damage to adjacent wall surfaces, requiring repair beyond simple cabinet replacement.

By 2024, the maintenance team had replaced approximately 40% of the original cabinets and repaired many others multiple times. The total investment in cabinet-related maintenance exceeded the original purchase price, yet problems continued. The ownership group recognized that a comprehensive replacement was necessary and was determined to select products that would end the replacement cycle rather than perpetuate it.

**Previous Solution Issues:**

The original medicine cabinets had been sourced from a regional supplier who offered competitive pricing but couldn't provide the long-term support the property needed. When problems emerged, the supplier's response was limited to replacing failed components under the 1-year warranty—after which the hotel was responsible for all costs. The supplier eventually went out of business, eliminating any possibility of factory support or replacement parts.

Replacement cabinets purchased from various sources over the years created additional problems. Non-matching dimensions required field modifications to fit existing wall openings, creating additional labor costs and aesthetic inconsistencies. Different hardware styles created visual mismatch within rooms and across the property. Varying quality levels meant some replacement cabinets began failing even before the original cabinets they replaced.

**Business Impact:**

The medicine cabinet situation created operational and reputational problems beyond direct maintenance costs. Guest reviews frequently mentioned bathroom quality issues, with specific complaints about dim lighting, difficult-to-open cabinets, and the general impression that the hotel wasn't maintaining its property appropriately. Staff time spent on cabinet repairs diverted attention from other guest service priorities. The inconsistent appearance of cabinets across the property undermined the quality image the hotel worked to maintain.

The ownership group calculated that cabinet-related issues were costing approximately $15,000-20,000 annually in direct maintenance, plus unquantified impact on guest satisfaction and repeat booking rates. The comprehensive renovation was an opportunity to resolve these issues definitively while upgrading other guest room elements.

### The HILO Solution

**Titre:**
> Why HILO Was Selected

**Selection Process:**

The Langford Hotel's renovation team evaluated medicine cabinet options from four suppliers, including both catalogue products and custom solutions. The evaluation criteria reflected the lessons learned from the property's 20-year cabinet experience: durability and expected lifespan, warranty coverage and supplier longevity, aesthetic appropriateness for the historic property, functionality and user experience, and pricing relative to total cost of ownership.

HILO was recommended by the hotel's interior designer, who had specified HILO products for other hospitality projects and was confident in their quality and support capabilities. The recommendation was validated through reference checks with comparable boutique properties and review of HILO's warranty terms and company stability.

HILO was selected based on several decisive factors. The 5-year comprehensive warranty covering structure, mirrors, hardware, and electrical components provided the long-term protection the ownership group wanted. The commercial-grade construction with moisture-resistant materials, solid surface shelves, and stainless steel hardware addressed the specific failure modes the property had experienced. The period-appropriate aesthetic options including beveled mirror frames and classic hardware styles complemented the hotel's historic character without appearing reproduction-grade. The integrated LED lighting with 50,000-hour rated components and tunable color temperature exceeded the performance of the original cabinets while meeting current energy efficiency requirements.

The pricing was competitive with alternatives offering inferior warranty coverage, making HILO the clear value choice for a project where total cost of ownership was the primary consideration.

The design maintained the clean lines appropriate for the building's 1920s architecture while providing contemporary functionality. The beveled mirror frame added a subtle period-appropriate detail that complemented the building's original millwork and hardware. The integrated lighting eliminated the need for separate vanity lighting, simplifying installation and reducing energy consumption.

**Key Differentiators:**

Several HILO capabilities proved particularly valuable for this boutique hotel application. The design consultation helped select a product that balanced contemporary performance with period-appropriate aesthetics. The phased delivery coordination aligned with the floor-by-floor renovation schedule, preventing storage issues and ensuring product availability when each phase began. The comprehensive warranty provided the long-term protection the ownership group needed to break the replacement cycle. The established company with 15+ years in business and 180+ completed projects provided confidence that warranty support would be available if needed.

### Implementation

**Titre:**
> Phased Renovation in an Operating Hotel

**Process Overview:**

Renovating a boutique hotel while maintaining operations requires meticulous planning and coordination. HILO's implementation approach was designed to support the floor-by-floor renovation schedule while minimizing disruption to hotel guests and operations.

The project began with detailed measurement of existing wall openings, which varied slightly across floors due to the building's age and previous modifications. HILO's technical team verified that the selected cabinet design would fit all openings or identified locations where minor modifications were needed. Production was scheduled to deliver cabinets in five phases aligned with each floor's renovation timeline, with each phase delivery arriving approximately one week before installation was scheduled to begin.

Installation was completed by the renovation contractor's carpentry team, with HILO providing detailed installation guidelines and phone support for any questions. The consistent product design simplified installation training, allowing the team to develop efficient routines that reduced installation time compared to the varied products used in previous piecemeal replacements.

**Coordination with Hotel Operations:**

Maintaining hotel operations during renovation required close coordination between the renovation team, hotel management, and HILO's logistics team. Delivery schedules were coordinated to avoid high-traffic periods and ensure that materials didn't create obstacles for guests or staff. Installation was timed to minimize noise impact on adjacent floors, with particularly loud work scheduled during periods of lower occupancy. The renovation team maintained positive relationships with front desk staff, keeping them informed about progress so they could appropriately manage guest expectations.

The phased approach also allowed the hotel to generate revenue from completed rooms while other floors remained under renovation, improving the project's financial profile compared to a full-property closure.

**Quality Verification:**

Each completed floor was inspected by the ownership group's representative before that floor returned to service. The inspection verified that all cabinets were properly installed, aligned, and functional. Lighting levels were tested to confirm consistent performance across rooms. Any issues identified were addressed immediately, with most being minor adjustments that could be completed within the same day.

The final floor completion marked the end of the 4-month renovation timeline, with all 156 cabinets installed and operational. The renovation team noted that the cabinet installation was one of the smoothest elements of the project, with no delays attributable to product availability or fit issues.

Guest feedback has been overwhelmingly positive since the renovation. Staff have also noticed the difference. Housekeeping staff report that the new cabinets are easier to clean and maintain, with smooth surfaces that don't trap dust or residue. The maintenance team has been able to redirect their attention from ongoing cabinet repairs to preventive maintenance and other priorities, improving overall property operations.

**Financial Impact:**

The financial benefits of the HILO investment extend beyond the avoided maintenance costs that motivated the project. The improvement in guest reviews has contributed to a 5% increase in direct bookings, reducing reliance on OTAs and improving average daily rate. The eliminated maintenance burden has allowed the ownership group to defer a planned staff increase, avoiding approximately $25,000 in annual labor costs.

The ownership group's analysis projects that the cabinets will remain in service for at least 10 years based on HILO's durability track record, compared to the 5-7 year average lifespan of the original cabinets. This extended service life, combined with the eliminated maintenance costs and improved guest experience, delivers a projected 5-year return of 3x the initial investment in HILO products compared to continued use of the original cabinets or budget-brand replacements.
