## Block 2: Definition Block

## What Is Commercial Shower Glass?

Commercial shower glass refers to tempered safety glass enclosures engineered for high-frequency use in hospitality, multi-family, and institutional bathrooms. Unlike residential shower doors, commercial-grade shower glass must withstand daily operation by hundreds of users, resist constant moisture exposure, and meet stringent safety certifications including ANSI Z97.1 and CPSC 16 CFR 1201. HILO shower glass systems encompass frameless, semi-frameless, and framed configurations in multiple glass thicknesses, hardware finishes, and enclosure styles — all backed by a comprehensive 5-year warranty covering glass panels, hardware, seals, and installation workmanship.

## Block 3: Criterion #1 — Fiabilité Long Terme (Long-Term Reliability)

## Why Budget Shower Glass Costs More in the Long Run

Shower glass seems straightforward — a few panels, some hardware, a door. Many contractors default to the lowest bidder, assuming all glass is essentially the same. This assumption is expensive. Budget shower glass installations fail in predictable ways. Hardware corrodes within 18 months from constant moisture exposure. Rollers seize up, making doors difficult to open and close. Seals degrade, allowing water to escape onto bathroom floors. Glass develops stress cracks from improper tempering or installation. Frameless panels shift out of alignment, creating gaps and binding doors. Each failure generates costs far beyond the original savings: maintenance tickets pull staff from other priorities, guest complaints damage review scores and repeat booking rates, emergency replacements cost 2-3x scheduled replacements, water damage from failed seals can affect flooring and subfloors, and liability exposure from broken glass or water hazards compounds the problem.

HILO shower glass systems are designed for the reality of daily commercial use. We specify tempered safety glass that meets or exceeds ANSI Z97.1 and CPSC 16 CFR 1201 standards. Our hardware is stainless steel or solid brass — never plated finishes that corrode under moisture. Our seals and rollers are rated for 100,000+ cycles. Across 180+ projects and thousands of installed shower enclosures, HILO has documented a glass breakage rate of less than 0.3% over 5 years. Hardware failure rate is similarly minimal. These are not aspirational goals — they are documented performance backed by real project data.

Our 5-year warranty covers every critical component without pro-rating. If a panel breaks in year four, you receive a full replacement at no cost. Glass panels are covered for breakage from normal use. Hinges, handles, rollers, and clamps are replaced if they fail. Water containment components including seals and sweeps are replaced when worn. Installation workmanship issues are repaired at no charge. We have engineered out the failure points that plague standard shower glass through fully tempered safety glass with edge finishing that prevents stress concentration, consistent thickness across each panel, quality verification at multiple production stages, hardware with load ratings exceeding actual door weights by 3x, and self-lubricating roller bearings with UV-stable silicone formulations rated for 10+ years.

## Block 13: FAQ Block

## Frequently Asked Questions About Commercial Shower Glass

### What shower glass thickness do I need for a commercial installation?

Commercial shower enclosures typically require either 3/8-inch or 1/2-inch tempered safety glass, depending on the enclosure type and panel dimensions. For standard inline doors and single panels up to 36 inches wide, 3/8-inch glass provides sufficient structural performance and is the most common specification for hospitality projects. For wider panels exceeding 36 inches, frameless designs, or ceiling-height installations, 1/2-inch glass delivers the additional rigidity needed for long-term stability. All HILO shower glass meets or exceeds ANSI Z97.1 and CPSC 16 CFR 1201 safety certification regardless of thickness, meaning both options provide equivalent safety performance. Our team can recommend the appropriate thickness based on your specific enclosure dimensions and usage profile.

### How long does it take to receive a shower glass order?

Lead times depend on whether you select catalogue or custom configurations. Standard catalogue enclosures ship within 2-4 weeks from order confirmation. Custom configurations requiring non-standard dimensions, special glass types, or unique hardware typically require 4-6 weeks. Production begins after your order is confirmed and, for custom orders, after field measurements are verified. Delivery across Canada and the United States is included in our standard pricing, and multi-site projects can be scheduled with staged deliveries that align to your installation sequence. Rush options are available for time-sensitive projects — contact our team to discuss expedited timelines.

### What does the 5-year shower glass warranty actually cover?

HILO's 5-year warranty is comprehensive and non-pro-rated. Glass panels are covered for breakage from normal use, meaning if a panel shatters without intentional impact or vandalism, we replace it at no cost. All hardware components including hinges, handles, rollers, and clamps are covered for functional failure. Seals and sweeps are covered when they degrade or lose water containment performance. Installation workmanship is covered when installed by HILO-certified partners. Claims are handled with a simple photo documentation process — we assess within 48 hours and ship replacements promptly. There are no pro-rated payouts: a panel that fails in year four receives the same full replacement as one that fails in month six.

### Can HILO shower glass be installed in existing bathrooms during renovations?

Yes, HILO shower glass is well-suited for renovation projects in existing bathrooms. Our standard catalogue configurations are designed to fit common rough opening dimensions found in commercial properties built over the past several decades. For older buildings or non-standard layouts, our custom program fabricates enclosures to exact field-measured dimensions. Our certified installation partners are experienced in renovation environments and work within occupied properties with appropriate dust containment and scheduling protocols. The measurement-first process ensures that glass is fabricated to actual field conditions, accounting for walls that may not be perfectly plumb or level — a common issue in renovation projects that generic suppliers often fail to accommodate.

### What is the difference between frameless, semi-frameless, and framed shower enclosures?

Framed enclosures use a metal frame around each glass panel, providing the most economical option and the simplest installation. The frame adds structural support and allows for thinner glass, but the visible metal reduces the open, contemporary aesthetic. Semi-frameless enclosures use minimal framing — typically a header bar or partial frame — to reduce visual obstruction while maintaining some of the structural simplicity and cost advantages of fully framed designs. Frameless enclosures use thick tempered glass panels secured with discrete hardware mounts, creating a nearly all-glass appearance that is the preferred choice for upscale hospitality and premium commercial projects. Frameless installations require precise wall conditions and are typically the most expensive option, but they deliver the most refined visual result and are the most requested configuration across HILO's 180+ projects.
