## Project Overview

**Titre:**
> About the Project

**Client Background:**

The Langford Hotel is a 78-room boutique property located in a historic building in the heart of a vibrant Canadian city. Originally constructed in 1928 as an office building, the property was converted to a boutique hotel in 2004, capitalizing on the building's historic character, prime location, and architectural details.

The hotel has built a loyal following among travelers who appreciate its blend of historic charm, contemporary amenities, and personalized service. The ownership group acquired the property in 2018 with a commitment to ongoing investment in guest experience and building systems.

**Project Scope:**

- All 78 guest rooms across five floors
- 156 medicine cabinets total (including suites with double vanities)
- Phased floor-by-floor renovation while hotel remained open
- Approximately 3 weeks per floor

**Key Requirements:**

- Historical appropriateness—complement 1920s character with contemporary function
- Durability—break the 20-year replacement cycle
- Integrated lighting—meet guest expectations and energy efficiency requirements
- Recessed installation—maximize counter space
- Phased delivery coordination

---

## The Challenge

**Titre:**
> The Challenge: Ending a 20-Year Replacement Cycle

**Problem Statement:**

The medicine cabinets installed during the hotel's 2004 conversion had become a persistent source of maintenance issues and guest complaints:

- Mirror delamination with expanding black edges
- Shelf sagging making cabinets functionally unusable
- Door alignment problems from loosened hinges
- Lighting failures with unavailable replacement parts
- Moisture infiltration damaging adjacent wall surfaces

By 2024, the maintenance team had replaced approximately 40% of original cabinets and repaired many others multiple times. Total investment in cabinet-related maintenance exceeded the original purchase price.

**Business Impact:**

- Guest reviews frequently mentioned bathroom quality issues
- Staff time diverted from other guest service priorities
- Inconsistent cabinet appearance across property
- Estimated $15,000-20,000 annually in direct maintenance costs

## The HILO Solution

**Titre:**
> Why HILO Was Selected

**Selection Process:**

The Langford Hotel's renovation team evaluated medicine cabinet options from four suppliers, including both catalogue products and custom solutions. Evaluation criteria reflected lessons learned from 20 years of cabinet experience.

**HILO Selected Based On:**

- 5-year comprehensive warranty (structure, mirrors, hardware, electrical)
- Commercial-grade construction (moisture-resistant materials, solid surface shelves, stainless steel hardware)
- Period-appropriate aesthetic options (beveled mirror frames, classic hardware styles)
- Integrated LED lighting (50,000-hour rated components, tunable color temperature)
- Competitive pricing with superior warranty coverage

**Products Selected:**

- 20" x 26" recessed cabinet body
- Beveled mirror door frame
- Integrated LED lighting with touch dimmer (3000K/4000K/5000K)
- Three adjustable glass shelves with polished edges
- Stainless steel soft-close hinges (polished chrome)
- Full perimeter moisture sealing
- Tempered safety glass mirror

## Implementation

**Titre:**
> Phased Renovation in an Operating Hotel

**Process Overview:**

- Detailed measurement of existing wall openings (variations across floors)
- Technical team verification of fit for all locations
- Production scheduled in five phases aligned with floor renovation timelines
- Installation by renovation contractor with HILO installation guidelines and phone support

**Coordination with Hotel Operations:**

- Delivery schedules coordinated to avoid high-traffic periods
- Installation timed to minimize noise impact on adjacent floors
- Front desk kept informed for guest expectation management
- Revenue maintained from completed rooms while other floors renovated

**Quality Verification:**

- Each completed floor inspected before returning to service
- Cabinets verified for installation, alignment, and functionality
- Lighting levels tested for consistency across rooms
- Issues addressed immediately (mostly minor adjustments)
