## Project Overview

**Titre:**
> About the Project

**Client Background:**

The client operates a portfolio of 45 mid-scale and upscale hotels across the United States and Canada, ranging from airport properties to downtown business hotels and suburban extended-stay locations. Their brand is built on consistent quality, reliable amenities, and competitive pricing for business and leisure travelers. With over 6,000 guest rooms under management, maintaining consistent standards across all properties is both a priority and a significant operational challenge.

The hotel chain had historically sourced mirrors from multiple regional suppliers, creating inconsistent quality and fragmented warranty management. Each property manager had autonomy over renovation decisions, resulting in a patchwork of mirror types, ages, and conditions across the portfolio.

**Project Scope:**

In 2024, the hotel chain initiated a comprehensive guest room refresh program covering all 45 properties. The program included new bedding, updated furniture, refreshed bathroom fixtures, and—critically—a complete replacement of all bathroom mirrors. The facilities team saw an opportunity to standardize on a single mirror supplier for the entire portfolio.

The initial scope called for approximately 3,200 LED mirrors across all properties, with additional potential for future renovations and new construction projects. The timeline was ambitious: complete installation across all 45 properties within 18 months.

**Key Requirements:**

- Durability—products that withstand daily hotel use for at least 5 years
- Aesthetic consistency—uniform illumination for guest experience and marketing
- Competitive pricing—within renovation budget without sacrificing quality
- Logistics capability—coordinate deliveries to 45 locations on varying schedules

---

## The Challenge

**Titre:**
> The Challenge: Mirror Failures Were Draining Resources

**Problem Statement:**

Before the HILO partnership, mirror-related maintenance was one of the top three sources of guest room maintenance tickets across the hotel portfolio. Analysis revealed that the average property generated 15-20 mirror-related work orders per month, spanning issues from lighting failures and electrical problems to physical damage, delamination, and mounting issues.

These maintenance calls created cascading operational problems: staff time to diagnose and resolve, parts sourcing with long lead times, guest rooms sometimes taken out of service, and disproportionate guest complaints and negative reviews.

**Business Impact:**

Across the portfolio, mirror-related maintenance was costing an estimated $180,000-220,000 annually in direct expenses alone. The indirect costs from guest dissatisfaction and operational disruption were harder to quantify but clearly significant.

## Implementation

**Titre:**
> Coordinated Rollout Across 45 Properties

**Process Overview:**

The 18-month rollout was structured in four phases aligned with renovation schedules:

| Phase | Properties | Duration |
|-------|------------|----------|
| Phase 1: Pilot | 3 properties | 8 weeks |
| Phase 2: Eastern Region | 15 properties | 5 months |
| Phase 3: Central/Western | 18 properties | 6 months |
| Phase 4: Completion | 9 properties | Variable |

**Logistics Coordination:**

- Dedicated delivery window for each property
- Shipments from Canada and US distribution facilities
- Custom crating for protection
- Real-time tracking for facilities team
- **Damage-free delivery:** 99.9% success rate (3 of 3,200 damaged, all replaced within 72 hours)

**Installation:**

- Average installation time: 35 minutes per mirror (vs. 45-60 minutes for previous products)
- Certified installation partners
- Documentation for each property
