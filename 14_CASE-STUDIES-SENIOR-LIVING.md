## Project Overview

**Titre:**
> About the Project

**Client Background:**

The client operates a continuum-of-care senior living community in Canada, offering independent living, assisted living, and memory care services across a campus of four interconnected buildings. The community is home to 320 residents ranging from active seniors in independent living apartments to those requiring comprehensive memory care support.

The organization has built its reputation on thoughtful design, advanced technology integration, and a resident-first approach that prioritizes dignity, independence, and quality of life.

**Project Scope:**

| Care Level | Product | Quantity |
|------------|---------|----------|
| Independent Living | Smart Mirrors | 185 units |
| Assisted Living | LED Mirrors | 150 units |
| Memory Care | LED Mirrors | 70 units |

**Key Requirements:**

- Absolute reliability—no technology failures that frustrate residents
- Ease of use—intuitive controls for varying cognitive abilities
- Durability—avoid consumer-grade replacement cycles
- Integration capability—connect with community systems
- Privacy-first approach—no cameras or always-listening devices

---

## The Challenge

**Titre:**
> The Challenge: Finding Technology That Serves Seniors

**Problem Statement:**

The senior living community had experienced disappointing technology implementations in previous years. Consumer-oriented smart home devices had proven unsuitable—designed for tech-savvy users, lacking durability for institutional settings, and creating ongoing maintenance challenges.

**Previous Solution Issues:**

- Consumer devices designed for home use, not institutional 24/7 operation
- Complex interfaces assuming technical familiarity
- Privacy concerns with cameras, voice assistants, always-on connectivity
- 1-year warranties with limited support options

**Business Impact:**

Aging bathroom amenities had become a competitive disadvantage. Prospective residents compared facilities during selection, and traditional mirrors didn't match modern amenities offered by newer competitors. Resident surveys identified bathroom lighting and mirrors as areas for improvement.

---

## The HILO Solution

**Titre:**
> Why HILO Was Selected

**Selection Process:**

The organization conducted rigorous evaluation with input from facilities team, IT department, resident council, and care staff. HILO was one of three finalists invited to present to the selection committee, which included resident representatives.

**HILO Selected Based On:**

- Commercial-grade construction (100,000-hour rated components)
- Simplified interface with large touch targets
- 5-year comprehensive warranty
- No cameras or always-listening voice assistants (privacy protection)
- References from two senior living communities with 3+ years of performance

**Products Selected:**

| Product | Features | Application |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| **HILO Smart Mirror Pro** | 21.5" HD display, weather/news, calendar integration, dining menus, personalized reminders, demister, adjustable LED | Independent Living |
| **HILO LED Mirror Plus** | Enhanced LED lighting, high CRI, large touch dimmer buttons, demister, motion sensor option | Assisted Living & Memory Care |

---

## Implementation

**Titre:**
> Thoughtful Deployment for Sensitive Environments

**Process Overview:**

- **Pilot Phase:** 10 units across different care levels, 6 weeks
- **Feedback Integration:** Larger font sizes, optimized control positioning
- **Building-by-Building Rollout:** Scheduled during resident activity times

**Resident Preparation:**

- Announcement letters explaining new mirrors
- Demonstration sessions in common areas
- Printed quick-start guides with large text
- One-on-one orientation for residents requesting assistance

**Staff Training:**

- Facilities team: Operation, troubleshooting, warranty claims
- Care staff: LED mirror features, assisting residents
- IT team: Smart mirror integration, content management
