## Project Overview

**Titre:**
> About the Project

**Client Background:**

The client is a regional real estate development company known for thoughtfully designed urban infill projects that combine residential, commercial, and retail spaces in walkable, community-oriented developments. Their portfolio includes seven completed mixed-use projects across major metropolitan areas.

The Urban Quarter development represents their most ambitious project to date: a 450,000 square foot mixed-use complex comprising:
- 280 residential units
- 85,000 square feet of Class A office space
- 25,000 square feet of street-level retail
- Extensive amenity areas including rooftop terrace, fitness center, coworking lounge, and event spaces

**Project Scope:**

| Application | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Glass partitions | Office demising walls, conference rooms |
| Glass railings | Residential corridors, amenity terraces, rooftop lounge |
| Glass canopies | Building entrances, walkway connections |
| Interior features | Three-story lobby feature wall, glass elevator lobbies |

**Total scope:** 15,000+ square feet of architectural glass

**Key Requirements:**

- Structural performance meeting/exceeding building codes
- Aesthetic consistency across applications
- Durability for long-term performance
- Coordination capability with multiple stakeholders
- Budget considerations secondary to quality

---

## The Challenge

**Titre:**
> The Challenge: Coordinating Complex Glass Systems at Scale

**Problem Statement:**

Mixed-use developments present unique challenges for architectural glass procurement. Unlike single-use projects where requirements are relatively uniform, this project demanded multiple glass systems with different specifications, performance requirements, and installation sequences.

**Previous Project Issues:**

- Glass partition systems required extensive field modifications
- Railing systems developed corrosion at attachment points within two years
- Glass supplier coordination failures created storage and scheduling problems
- Canopy seal failures caused water infiltration

**Business Impact:**

The development represented significant capital investment and flagship positioning. Glass systems were highly visible design elements that would shape first impressions. Failures would undermine premium positioning and economics.

## Implementation

**Titre:**
> Complex Coordination Across Multiple Phases

**Process Overview:**

| Phase | Focus | Duration |
|-------|-------|----------|
| 1 | Pre-construction coordination | Planning |
| 2 | Early installations (canopies, exterior railings) | Before facade |
| 3 | Residential railings, lobby features | Interior fit-out |
| 4 | Office partition systems | Core-and-shell completion |
| 5 | Rooftop railings, amenity partitions | Project completion |

**Coordination Excellence:**

- Dedicated senior project manager as primary contact
- Engineering documentation submitted ahead of schedule
- Delivery scheduling integrated with overall logistics plan
- 24-48 hour response for field condition modifications

**Quality Control:**

- Factory inspections before shipping
- On-site installation verification
- Documentation for each installation
- Final punch list: fewer than 30 items across all installations

## Results & Metrics

**Titre:**
> Recognition and Performance

**Quantitative Results:**

| Metric | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Glass breakages | **Zero** across all systems |
| Hardware issues | Minor adjustments only, no failures |
| Water infiltration | **Zero** (canopy systems) |
| Glass-related maintenance requests (18 months) | 12 total (~0.1 per system) |

**Design Recognition:**

The Urban Quarter received the local chapter of the American Institute of Architects' **Design Excellence Award**, with specific recognition for "thoughtful integration of glass systems that create transparency and connection while respecting the urban context."

**Financial Impact:**

- Rental rates exceed initial projections by **8-10%** (residential and office)
- Strong tenant retention in office component
- Reduced reserve requirements for capital improvements
- HILO specified for two additional projects in development
