### Section 1: The Hidden Problem with Medicine Cabinets

**Titre:**
> The Medicine Cabinet Problem No One Talks About

**Copy:**

Medicine cabinets seem simple. A box with doors, a mirror, some shelves. What could go wrong?

Plenty, as it turns out. In hospitality and multi-family projects, medicine cabinets fail in predictable ways: doors that won't close after six months of humidity exposure, shelves that sag under the weight of actual guest belongings, mirrors that delaminate from moisture infiltration, and hardware that corrodes from daily bathroom conditions.

These failures don't happen immediately. They appear 18-24 months after installation, right when warranty periods expire and replacement costs fall squarely on property owners. A single sagging shelf becomes a maintenance ticket. A door that won't close becomes a guest complaint. A delaminated mirror becomes an expensive replacement project requiring wall repair and re-painting.

HILO medicine cabinets are engineered to prevent every one of these common failure modes. We use moisture-resistant materials, heavy-duty hardware, and tempered glass mirrors that won't delaminate. The result? Cabinets that look and function like new after 5+ years of daily use.

**Common Failure Modes (That HILO Prevents):**

| Failure | Industry Standard | HILO Solution |
|---------|------------------|---------------|
| Sagging shelves | Particle board | Solid surface / Moisture-resistant MDF |
| Door misalignment | Standard hinges | Soft-close, adjustable hinges |
| Mirror delamination | Standard adhesive | Tempered glass + sealed edges |
| Hardware corrosion | Chrome-plated | Stainless steel or solid brass |
| Moisture damage | Basic sealant | Full perimeter sealing |

### Section 1: The True Cost of Cheap Shower Glass

**Titre:**
> Why Budget Shower Glass Costs More in the Long Run

**Copy:**

Shower glass seems straightforward—a few panels, some hardware, a door. Many contractors default to the lowest bidder, assuming all glass is essentially the same. This assumption is expensive.

Budget shower glass installations fail in predictable ways. Hardware corrodes within 18 months from constant moisture exposure. Rollers seize up, making doors difficult to open and close. Seals degrade, allowing water to escape onto bathroom floors. Glass develops stress cracks from improper tempering or installation. Frameless panels shift out of alignment, creating gaps and binding doors.

Each failure generates costs far beyond the original "savings":

- **Maintenance tickets** pull staff from other priorities
- **Guest complaints** damage review scores and repeat booking rates
- **Emergency replacements** cost 2-3x scheduled replacements
- **Water damage** from failed seals can affect flooring and subfloors
- **Liability exposure** from broken glass or water hazards

HILO shower glass systems are designed for the reality of daily hospitality use. We specify tempered safety glass that meets or exceeds ANSI Z97.1 and CPSC 16 CFR 1201 standards. Our hardware is stainless steel or solid brass—never plated finishes that corrode. Our seals and rollers are rated for 100,000+ cycles.

**Failure Cost Comparison:**

| Cost Category | Budget Glass | HILO Glass |
|---------------|--------------|------------|
| Initial installation | Lower | Competitive |
| Maintenance calls (5 years) | 8-12 per unit | 0-1 per unit |
| Hardware replacement | Likely in 2-3 years | Covered by warranty |
| Glass replacement | 5-8% failure rate | <0.3% failure rate |
| Guest complaint impact | Negative reviews | Neutral to positive |
| **5-Year Total Cost** | **3-4x initial cost** | **1.1x initial cost** |
