## Block 14 — FAQ Block

### Frequently Asked Questions About Smart Mirrors

#### How do smart mirrors differ from LED mirrors?

LED mirrors provide integrated backlighting around the mirror surface for improved illumination during grooming tasks. Smart mirrors build on the LED mirror platform by adding an HD display panel behind a semi-reflective glass surface, enabling interactive functionality such as weather displays, news feeds, property information, Bluetooth audio, and optional hotel PMS integration. While LED mirrors focus on lighting quality and reliability, smart mirrors add a technology layer that creates an interactive guest experience. Both product lines share HILO's commercial-grade construction standards, tempered safety glass, and 5-year warranty. The primary trade-off is that smart mirrors cost more due to the display technology and require network connectivity for full functionality, while LED mirrors offer simpler installation and lower cost with no connectivity requirements.

#### Can smart mirrors work without WiFi?

Yes, HILO smart mirrors are designed to function as fully capable illuminated mirrors even without network connectivity. The LED lighting, demister pad, clock, and basic display features all operate independently of WiFi or Ethernet connections. When network connectivity is available, the smart mirror can access real-time information such as weather updates and, with PMS integration enabled, communicate with the property management system. If connectivity is lost, the mirror gracefully degrades to its core lighting and display functions without any error messages or guest-facing issues. This resilient design approach ensures that a network outage never results in a completely non-functional fixture—which is exactly the kind of reliability that commercial environments demand.

#### How difficult is smart mirror installation compared to standard LED mirrors?

Smart mirror installation requires the same basic mounting procedure as LED mirrors but adds a few additional steps. Electrical connections are identical—power is supplied through a standard 120V junction box behind the mirror. The additional requirements include network connectivity setup (either WiFi configuration or Ethernet cable routing), initial display calibration for optimal viewing in the specific bathroom lighting conditions, and optional PMS integration configuration. For properties using HILO's certified installation partners, these additional steps add approximately fifteen to twenty minutes per unit compared to a standard LED mirror installation. The total installation time for a smart mirror typically ranges from thirty to forty-five minutes per unit, making it entirely practical for large-scale property rollouts.

#### What happens when the display technology becomes outdated?

HILO smart mirrors are designed with long-term commercial use in mind, using display technology rated for 100,000 hours of operation—approximately eleven years of continuous use. The core functionality of displaying information, providing touch interaction, and integrating with hotel systems relies on proven, stable technologies that are not subject to the rapid obsolescence cycles of consumer electronics. The hardened operating system is designed for extended commercial deployment with security updates and feature stability over the full product lifecycle. When the time eventually comes to refresh the technology, HILO's modular construction approach allows for display panel replacement without removing the entire mirror assembly, reducing the cost and disruption of technology upgrades compared to replacing complete units.

#### Are smart mirrors worth the investment for mid-scale hotel properties?

The value proposition of smart mirrors for mid-scale properties depends on the competitive positioning strategy of the specific hotel. For properties competing on guest experience and seeking to differentiate themselves from similarly priced competitors, smart mirrors in suites and upgraded rooms create a memorable touchpoint that guests notice and remember. The competitive pricing of HILO smart mirrors has made the technology accessible to mid-scale properties that previously could not justify the investment. When installed selectively in premium room categories rather than in every room, the per-project investment becomes quite manageable while still delivering meaningful guest experience improvements. Additionally, the 0.3% five-year failure rate and comprehensive warranty protection mean that the ongoing maintenance cost is negligible—unlike lower-cost alternatives that may require frequent attention from hotel engineering teams.
