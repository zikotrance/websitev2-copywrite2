## Block 2 — Definition Block

Medicine cabinets are wall-mounted or recessed storage units with mirrored or semi-transparent doors designed for commercial bathroom environments including hotels, multi-family residential developments, senior living communities, and institutional facilities. Commercial medicine cabinets differ from residential models in their requirement for heavy-duty construction that withstands continuous daily use, high-humidity bathroom conditions, and the increased wear that comes from multiple occupants over the life of a building. HILO medicine cabinets feature moisture-resistant MDF construction, stainless steel or solid brass hardware, tempered glass mirrors with sealed edges, and adjustable shelving systems engineered to resist the sagging, corrosion, and delamination that commonly afflict standard commercial medicine cabinets within two to three years of installation.

## Block 14 — FAQ Block

### Frequently Asked Questions About Medicine Cabinets

#### What is the difference between surface mount and recessed medicine cabinets?

Surface mount medicine cabinets install directly onto the wall surface, requiring no cutting into the wall cavity. They are ideal for renovations where the wall cannot be opened, concrete or masonry wall construction, and situations where the rough-in was not planned during framing. Surface mount cabinets project approximately four to six inches from the wall surface. Recessed medicine cabinets install inside the wall cavity, creating a flush or near-flush appearance that provides a cleaner, more built-in aesthetic. Recessed installation requires an appropriately sized opening in the wall and is best suited for new construction or gut renovations where wall framing can be planned around the cabinet dimensions. HILO offers both mounting types in a wide range of sizes, and the choice between them depends on your project type, wall construction, and design preference.

#### Can medicine cabinets be installed in bathrooms without existing electrical connections?

Yes, HILO medicine cabinets are available in both non-lighted and LED-lighted configurations. Non-lighted models require no electrical connection and can be installed by any competent carpenter or handyman as a straightforward wall-mounted fixture. LED-lighted models require a power connection, typically supplied by a 120V junction box installed in the wall behind or above the cabinet during the rough-in electrical phase. For renovations where adding electrical connections is impractical, non-lighted cabinets provide the same storage functionality and quality construction without any wiring requirements. If you want the appearance of integrated lighting but lack existing electrical connections, consult with an electrician about adding a junction box during the cabinet installation—it is often a straightforward addition that can be completed in the same visit.

#### How do I determine the right size medicine cabinet for my project?

The right medicine cabinet size depends on several factors including the vanity width, wall opening dimensions (for recessed models), mirror surface requirements, and storage capacity needs. A common guideline is to select a cabinet that is approximately two-thirds the width of the vanity below it, though this is a design preference rather than a requirement. For commercial hotel projects, twenty-inch and twenty-four-inch widths are the most commonly specified sizes, providing adequate storage for guest personal items while fitting standard vanity layouts. For multi-family residential projects, the choice depends on bathroom size and target market—larger cabinets in primary bathrooms and smaller units in powder rooms or secondary bathrooms. HILO offers sizes from twelve inches to thirty-six inches across our four mounting types, and our AI estimation tool can help you compare options for different room types within your project.

#### Are HILO medicine cabinets ADA compliant?

Select HILO medicine cabinet models are designed to meet ADA accessibility requirements for mounting height and reach range. ADA compliance for medicine cabinets primarily concerns the installation height of the mirror surface and the operable parts such as handles and shelves, ensuring they are accessible to individuals using wheelchairs. The specific requirements include a maximum mounting height for the mirror surface and operable hardware, and adequate clear floor space for approach. When specifying medicine cabinets for ADA-compliant facilities such as senior living communities or accessible hotel rooms, consult with our team about which models meet the specific requirements of your project and local jurisdiction. We can provide detailed specification sheets and coordinate with your architect to ensure compliance.

#### What finish options are available for medicine cabinet hardware?

HILO medicine cabinets are available in a range of hardware finishes designed to coordinate with contemporary commercial bathroom design trends. Standard finish options include white (for painted cabinet bodies), satin nickel, matte black, and brushed gold. Custom finish options are available for projects that require specific colors or metallic tones to match existing bathroom fixtures and hardware. Stainless steel hinges and hardware are standard across all finish options, providing consistent corrosion resistance regardless of the cosmetic finish selected. When ordering, specify the desired finish for each component—cabinet body color, hardware finish, and mirror frame finish—to ensure complete coordination with your bathroom design specification.
